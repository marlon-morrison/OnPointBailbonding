What exactly is a bail bond?

A Bail bond is a percentage of bail paid to the bond agency so they can pay the remaining amount and the person may be released from jail to wait for their court date. Every state has its own bail bond rules and processes, but the basics are the same in every system. Typically, the amount paid to the bond agency is ten to fifteen percent of the original bail amount. The amount is determined by the seriousness of the crime and the criminal history of the person needing bail. For example, someone with federal charges may be required to pay fifteen or even twenty percent while someone with a very limited criminal history and lesser offense may only be required to pay ten percent.
